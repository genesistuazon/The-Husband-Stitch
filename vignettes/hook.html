The Girl Who Ignored Her Intuition

[179] There’s a classic, a real classic, that I haven’t told you yet. [180] A girlfriend and a boyfriend went parking. Some people say that means kissing in a car, but I know the story. I was there. They were parked on the edge of a lake. They were turning around in the back seat as if the world was moments from ending. Maybe it was. She offered herself and he took it, and after it was over, they turned on the radio.
[181] The voice on the radio announced that a mad, hook-handed murderer had escaped from a local insane asylum. The boyfriend chuckled as he flipped to a music station. As the song ended, the girlfriend heard a thin scratching sound, like a paperclip over glass. She looked at her boyfriend and then pulled her cardigan over her bare shoulders, wrapping one arm around her breasts.
[182] “We should go,” she said.
[183] “No, baby,” the boyfriend said. “Let’s go again.”
[184] “What if the killer comes here?” The girl asked. “The insane asylum is very close.”
[185] “We’ll be fine, baby,” the boyfriend said. “Don’t you trust me?”
[186] The girlfriend nodded reluctantly.
[187] Well then, he said, his voice trailing off in that way she would come to know so well. He took her hand off her chest and placed it onto himself. She finally looked away from the lakeside.
[188] Outside, the moonlight glinted off the shiny steel hook. The killer waved at her, grinning.

This story ends with the demise of a woman who disregarded her intuition. In the beginning, the woman’s intelligence was obvious; she was able to determine the kind of danger she was in and how they could prevent that from happening. However, because of her devotion to her paramour, she ignored this knowledge and proceeded with his decision, leading to her death. This sequence serves as a direct parallel to the protagonist’s eventual death. The boyfriend in the story parallels the protagonist’s husband; they are both adamant on getting pleasure from their partner so much so that they are willing to risk their partner’s life to get it. Similarly, both women demonstrate a tragic compliance, prioritizing their partners' demands over their own survival instincts. For the protagonist, she has full consciousness that her submission—the untying of her ribbon—will lead to a lethal outcome, yet she proceeds because it is the ultimate fulfillment of her husband’s desire.
